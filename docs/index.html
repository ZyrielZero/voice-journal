Voice Journal — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Voice Journal is a fully offline application. It is built so that your data never leaves your device, and this is enforced by the operating system: the app does not request the INTERNET permission, so it is technically incapable of sending anything anywhere.

What we collect

Nothing. Voice Journal does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal information. There are no analytics, no advertising, no crash reporting, no accounts, and no third-party services of any kind.

Where your data lives

Everything you create — audio recordings, transcripts, and their search index — is stored only in the app's private storage on your device. Recording, transcription, and search all run locally on the device using on-device models. None of it is uploaded, backed up to a server, or made accessible to anyone but you.

Permissions

The app requests microphone access solely to record the entries you choose to record, and a foreground-service permission so recording can continue with the screen off. These permissions are used only for that purpose and never to move data off the device.

Deleting your data

Deleting an entry removes it from the device. Uninstalling the app removes all of its data. Because nothing is ever sent off the device, there is no server-side copy to request or erase.

Children's privacy

Voice Journal does not collect data from anyone, including children.
